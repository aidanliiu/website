NBA MVP Model Predictions

Jul – Sep 2023

Overview

Through Inspirit AI's research program in the summer of 2023, I built a machine learning model to predict NBA MVP award outcomes. Sports analytics is a domain I genuinely love, so this project sat at the intersection of two things I care about: basketball and ML.

The project was eventually published in The Curieux Review (January 2026), hosted on GitHub, and submitted to the Journal of Student Research.

The model

I built a regression model that takes season-long per-game statistics — points, assists, rebounds, win shares, VORP, and several advanced metrics — as input and outputs a predicted MVP vote share for each player.

The training data covered roughly 20 seasons of MVP voting results and corresponding player stats. After experimenting with linear regression, gradient boosting, and a neural net approach, a gradient-boosted model (XGBoost) produced the best cross-validated results, achieving an R² of around 0.82 on historical vote shares.

Interesting findings

Narrative matters as much as stats. The model struggled on years where a "narrative" MVP (like Nikola Jokić in 2022) won over a player with statistically comparable numbers from a higher-seeded team. Win percentage of the player's team turned out to be one of the strongest features — voters heavily reward players on winning teams.

Points per game is actually somewhat overweighted by voters relative to efficiency metrics. When I trained a "voter-adjusted" model versus an "optimal" model, the optimal model consistently rated more efficient players higher than the actual voting did.

Publication & next steps

The paper was submitted to the Journal of Student Research and is currently awaiting a response. Writing for peer review required a level of rigor I hadn't encountered before — every claim needed to be backed by data, every methodology choice had to be justified.

If I were to extend this project, I'd want to incorporate social media sentiment and media coverage data — testing whether MVP buzz is predictable from Twitter/X volume in the weeks leading up to the announcement.
