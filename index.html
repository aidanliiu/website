Why me?

I show up for the team. As Captain and Mechanical Lead of our VEX Robotics team, I've led builds, driven competitions, and mentored newer members. As Chapter Director of Art4Hearts International and a teacher at the International Robotics Academy, I bring the same hands-on, structure-first approach to community work that I bring to research and engineering — clear plans, real reps, no fluff.

My story

I grew up splitting my time between the basketball court, the workshop, and the classroom — and I've never wanted to give any of them up. Basketball taught me discipline and how to perform under pressure; robotics taught me how to build, break, and rebuild things until they work; research gave me a way to ask bigger questions and actually dig for answers.

Outside of school I picked up weightlifting in 2025 (PRs: bench 185 lbs × 6, squat 255 lbs × 4, deadlift 255 lbs × 5) and played violin for six years growing up. I'm fluent in Mandarin, proficient in Spanish, and comfortable in Java, Python, C++, and HTML.

Whatever I'm working on — a research paper, a robot, a club, or a team — I try to bring the same mindset: build the structure first, put in the reps, and don't overcomplicate what doesn't need it.
